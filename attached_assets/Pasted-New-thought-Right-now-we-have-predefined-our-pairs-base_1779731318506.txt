Pair Selection Analysis — Plain English
How this was researched
Live data pulled directly from MEXC (777 pairs), Hyperliquid (230 pairs), and exchange APIs for real-time tick sizes, order book depth, open interest, and 24-hour volume — checked today at current prices.

The single most useful metric for identifying consolidation-prone pairs is the OI-to-Volume ratio — how much open interest is sitting on the exchange relative to how much actually trades in a day. A high ratio means lots of positions are parked and waiting. Low turnover relative to size = price tends to sit in ranges rather than trend. That is exactly what the ZW detector needs.
